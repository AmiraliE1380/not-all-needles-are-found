- Earth's only natural satellite, about 3,474 km in diameter (≈1/4 Earth's), orbiting at an average distance of ~384,400 km and formed ~4.5 billion years ago (likely from a giant impact).  
- Tidally locked so the same face points toward Earth, it produces lunar phases and drives Earth’s ocean tides; it also causes solar and lunar eclipses.  
- Virtually airless with extreme temperature swings, the Moon has been visited by humans (six crewed landings, 1969–1972) and retains a record of the early solar system on its surface.
