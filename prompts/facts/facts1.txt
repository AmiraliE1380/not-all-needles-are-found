She was born in the same city, Paris, in the same year as Augustine, and grew up alongside her. She married very early at 16, to a man exactly twice her age, and moved to Albi with him immediately afterward. The last time Augustine and Élodie-Rose saw each other was at Élodie-Rose’s wedding. Élodie-Rose became single again very shortly after and had to continue her life in a town far from her family. She endured a life full of hardship. She aged very quickly—her dark black hair turned gray, she developed uterine cancer, and her beautiful hazel eyes grew sad. She lived in Albi for two decades, most of it alone, and died there a lonely death.
