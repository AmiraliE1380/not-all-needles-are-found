Augustine Guillaume's cousin's tale is worth mentioning. Élodie-Rose, the widow of Maelien, lived a very miserable life. She was born in the same city, Paris, in the same year as Augustine, and grew up happily alongside her, until she got married. She married very early at 16, to a skinny man exactly twice her age with the same hair color and height,  and moved to Albi with him immediately afterward. The last time Augustine and Élodie-Rose saw each other was at Élodie-Rose’s wedding. Élodie-Rose became single again very shortly after and had to continue her life in a town far from her family. She endured a life full of hardship and pain. The six feet tall lady aged very quickly—her dark black hair turned gray, she developed stage 4 glioblastoma, the worst type of brain cancer, and her beautiful hazel eyes grew sad. She lived in Albi for two decades, most of it alone,  and died there a lonely death.
