**The Message**  
*By Honoré De Balzac*  
*Translated by Ellen Marriage*  

In this narrative, the author presents a poignant story that intertwines fear and love among young lovers, prompting them to seek comfort in one another. The tale merges personal experience with poetic potential.

In 1819, while traveling from Paris to Moulins, the narrator finds himself outside a coach due to financial constraints. He befriends a wealthier fellow traveler, and they bond over discussions of love, particularly for women aged thirty-five to forty. Their conversation is filled with youthful exuberance and romantic ideals, leading them to share intimate details about their mistresses, both older than themselves.

As they recount their romantic escapades, the young men express admiration for their devoted mistresses, despite their marriages. Their camaraderie deepens as they share the risks taken to be with their lovers. However, their journey takes a tragic turn when the coach overturns, resulting in the death of the narrator's companion.

In his final moments, the young man, trapped beneath the coach, entrusts the narrator with a solemn task: to inform his mistress of his death. He gives the narrator a key to access his home and the letters he wishes to return to her. The young man dies, leaving the narrator with the heavy burden of delivering the news.

Upon reaching La Charité, the narrator fulfills his friend's last wishes, encountering grief from the household staff but focusing on the sorrow awaiting the countess. With limited funds, he decides to walk the remaining distance to the Countess de Montpersan's chateau, driven by urgency to deliver the tragic news.

As he approaches the chateau, he grapples with the emotional weight of his task, realizing the cruelty of being the bearer of such devastating news. Upon arrival, he finds the chateau bustling but struggles to locate the countess. A young girl assists him, leading him through the park to find her mother.

This story captures the complexities of love, loss, and the burdens of communication, illustrating how a simple journey can transform into a profound encounter with fate. The narrator's reflections on youth, romance, and the inevitability of tragedy emphasize the delicate balance between joy and sorrow in human relationships.

I took great care to present myself well before meeting Juliette and her husband, M. le Comte and Mme. la Comtesse de Montpersan, wanting to avoid being mistaken for a local rate collector. Reflecting on that moment, I find it amusing.

As I composed myself, I spotted Juliette with her husband. She seemed hurried, and upon noticing me, her expression revealed disappointment. I struggled to recall the elegant phrases I had prepared, which allowed her husband to approach. I awkwardly inquired if they were indeed the Count and Countess. Observing them closely, I noted the husband’s pompous demeanor and shabby appearance, contrasting sharply with the Countess's delicate beauty and grace. She was petite, charming, and exuded youthfulness, evoking a strong desire within me.

Realizing the situation, I decided to inform the Count of my purpose. I discreetly mentioned the death of my traveling companion, which visibly affected him. He expressed concern for his wife, and I assured him I would not disclose the message I carried until he was informed. This earned me his trust, and we returned to the house where I was invited to dinner.

As we approached, Juliette cast a curious glance at us, revealing her mixed feelings about my unexpected presence. I felt sympathy for her, recalling her beauty in the sunlight. I revealed that I had come on behalf of someone who called her Juliette, which startled her. When I mentioned her companion's illness, she grew anxious, fearing for his life. Her desperate plea for the truth struck me deeply, and I could only nod in response.

Just then, the Count called for dinner, and I followed him inside. The meal was lavish, but the Countess was notably absent. A maid reported that she could not be found, prompting concern. The canon and I searched the grounds, calling for her, but to no avail. Eventually, we discovered her hidden in a hayloft, sobbing in despair. With the maid's help, we brought her to her room, instructing that she not be disturbed.

Upon returning to the dining room, I found the Count surprisingly unfazed, having consumed most of the meal. His indifference stemmed from a severe dietary restriction due to a medical condition, explaining his lack of concern for his wife’s absence. The evening was somber, filled with unanswered questions about Juliette's distress.

Later that night, I was awakened by the Countess, who appeared at my bedside, pale and shaken. She sought confirmation of the tragic news, and I recounted the accident, careful to spare her the most harrowing details. She listened intently, her grief palpable. When I revealed her companion's fears and the message he entrusted to me, her composure faltered, and she expressed regret for having burned his letters, feeling she had lost everything.

In a moment of connection, I handed her a lock of his hair, a tangible reminder of their bond. Her gratitude was overwhelming, and she wished me happiness in love before retreating with her precious token.

The following day felt surreal as I reflected on the previous night. I spent time with Juliette, witnessing her noble spirit and selflessness. In the evening, the Count accompanied me partway to Moulins, asking me to deliver a sum of money to a contact in Paris. I willingly accepted, unaware of the thoughtful gesture behind her request.

This experience deepened my understanding of love and sacrifice, leaving me with a poignant story to share with a woman who cherished me, reminding me of the fragility of life and the bonds we form.
