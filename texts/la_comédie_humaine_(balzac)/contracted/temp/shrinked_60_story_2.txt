The Comte de Fontaine, a royalist from Poitou, faced financial ruin during the French Revolution when his estate was confiscated. He married Mademoiselle de Kergarouet and moved to Paris, seeking royal favor. After initially feeling unrecognized, he became a loyal servant to Louis XVIII, securing positions for his sons.

Despite their improved status, the Comte struggled to find suitable husbands for his three daughters, particularly the youngest, Emilie, whose high standards and spoiled nature made her difficult to please. Her sense of superiority led her to dismiss many suitors. Emilie's beauty and pride alienated potential matches, and her father realized too late that her upbringing fostered entitlement.

Emilie met Maximilien Longueville, whose elegance intrigued her. As they grew closer, her affection deepened despite her father's concerns about his unclear status. However, her idealized image of him shattered when she discovered he worked in a shop, leading to emotional turmoil. Ultimately, she married the Comte de Kergarouet but remained haunted by memories of Longueville and her choices.
