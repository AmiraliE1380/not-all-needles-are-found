The Marquise de Listomere represents the Restoration's blend of religious devotion and worldly elegance. Married for seven years to the unremarkable Marquis de Listomere, she maintains a virtuous image to support his political ambitions. Known as "dull weather," the marquis provides a stable home life that aligns with her values.

Despite her reserved demeanor, the Marquise commands respect and skillfully deflects flirtatious advances, engaging in scandal-free conversations with agreeable men. Her beauty is subtle, revealing depth when animated.

Recently, she danced with Eugène de Rastignac, a charming young man. Their conversation, which shifted from light topics to women's roles, left a lasting impression. Rastignac, captivated by the Marquise, chose not to pursue her further that evening.

The next morning, Rastignac reflected on the Marquise while writing letters, interrupted by his friend Horace discussing an expedition to Morea. Meanwhile, the Marquise received a letter from Rastignac. Initially inclined to discard it, she read on, feeling fatigue and intrigue, which challenged her perceptions of propriety.

Madame de Listomere resolved to forbid her porter from admitting Rastignac, ultimately burning the love letter, surprising her housemaid, Caroline.

At a party hosted by the Marquis de Beauseant, she anticipated Rastignac's arrival. By 2 AM, realizing he would not come, her thoughts underwent a process Stendhal termed "crystallization."

Days later, Rastignac discovered a misplaced letter for Madame de Listomere, reflecting on his mistakes. When he finally visited, the porter informed him she was out, but the marquis invited him in. Rastignac noticed her blush, flattering his vanity.

Their conversation revealed underlying tensions, with her skepticism about his intentions. When Rastignac inadvertently suggested another woman was the intended recipient of the letter, she dismissed him coldly.

Shortly after, Madame de Listomere withdrew from society, claiming illness, while the marquis attributed her absence to stomach inflammation. In reality, she experienced a nervous attack, using it as an excuse to avoid social interactions, highlighting the complexities of romantic pursuits among the young and inexperienced.
