The Marquise de Listomere represents the Restoration's blend of religious devotion and worldly elegance. Married for seven years to the unremarkable Marquis de Listomere, she maintains a virtuous image to support his political ambitions. Known as "dull weather," the marquis provides a stable home life that aligns with her values.

Despite her reserved demeanor, the Marquise commands respect and skillfully deflects flirtatious advances. Her beauty is subtle, revealing depth when animated. After dancing with Eugène de Rastignac, a charming young man, she is captivated but he chooses not to pursue her further.

The next morning, Rastignac reflects on the Marquise while writing letters, interrupted by his friend Horace. Meanwhile, the Marquise receives a letter from Rastignac, which intrigues her despite her initial impulse to discard it, highlighting her internal conflict. She resolves to forbid her porter from admitting him, ultimately burning the letter, surprising her maid, Caroline. 

At a party hosted by the Marquis de Beauseant, she anticipates Rastignac's arrival but feels disappointed when he does not come. Days later, Rastignac learns he mistakenly sent a love letter meant for another to Madame de Listomere. When he visits, the marquis invites him in, and Rastignac notices the Marquise blush, revealing romantic tensions. Later, she withdraws from society, claiming illness, while the marquis masks her nervousness as stomach issues.
