Felix Gaudissart, a commercial traveler selling Parisian hats, embodies a shift from materialism to intellectual pursuits. Charming and accustomed to a lavish life, he turns after the 1830 upheaval to life insurance and journalism to meet public demand for new ideas. While training and writing he falls for the florist Jenny, promising to furnish her room with his earnings. In Vouvray he meets skepticism and indifference; his encounter with Monsieur Vernier, who mocks him, leads to a comic exchange with Margaritis, a local eccentric. As townspeople plot to humiliate him, Gaudissart accepts a duel that proves farcical: on the day both men see their quarrel's absurdity and reconcile over breakfast. He learns humility, discovering that Parisian charm won’t guarantee provincial success, illustrating Balzac’s themes of commerce and human nature. These characters reappear in Balzac’s Human Comedy.
