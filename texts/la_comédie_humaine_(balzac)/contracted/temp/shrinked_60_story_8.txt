In September 1835, Mademoiselle du Rouvre married Polish exile Comte Adam Mitgislas Laginski. Despite her father's financial troubles, Clementine inherited wealth from her mother’s family. Initially perceived as frivolous, Adam gained respect after buying a house in Paris, becoming a social figure by 1836. Captain Thaddeus Paz, a nobleman and their financial manager, developed unrequited feelings for Clementine, complicating their relationships.

Thaddeus became infatuated with circus performer Malaga, creating a distraction from his feelings for Clementine. Their relationship deteriorated when Malaga confronted him. As Thaddeus cared for Adam during an illness, Clementine began to appreciate his management skills. 

During carnival season, Thaddeus proposed a night out with Malaga, leading to a confrontation with Clementine, who demanded he end it. Ultimately, Thaddeus chose to leave Paris for an expedition, believing it best for all. After his departure, Clementine recognized her feelings for him, while Thaddeus expressed his unrequited love in a letter. Three years later, Clementine remained intrigued by Thaddeus, highlighting the complexities of love and societal expectations.
