Honoré De Balzac's narrative delves into fear and love among young lovers. In 1819, the narrator, unable to afford a coach, befriends a wealthier traveler, and they bond over their admiration for older mistresses. Tragedy strikes when the coach overturns, killing the narrator's companion, who tasks him with informing the Countess de Montpersan of his death. Burdened, the narrator walks to her chateau, struggling to find her amid the chaos. After discreetly informing the Count, he gains his trust. The Countess, upon learning of her companion's illness, is devastated. The narrator spares her painful details and gives her a lock of hair as a reminder. Reflecting on the experience, he gains insight into love and sacrifice.
