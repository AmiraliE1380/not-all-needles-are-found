De Balzac dedicates "The Vicar of Tours" to sculptor David, emphasizing the permanence of art over fleeting writing. In 1826, Abbe Birotteau, a priest seeking a canon nomination, lives with Mademoiselle Gamard but feels neglected and suspects her ill will. He discovers her plot against him, naively signs away his belongings, and realizes Troubert has taken over his apartment. After Gamard's death, her fortune strengthens Troubert's power while Birotteau's life declines in illness and despair.
