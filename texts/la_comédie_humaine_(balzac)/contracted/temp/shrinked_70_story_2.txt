The Comte de Fontaine, a royalist from Poitou, faced financial ruin during the French Revolution and sought royal favor in Paris after marrying Mademoiselle de Kergarouet. Despite securing positions for his sons, he struggled to find husbands for his daughters, especially Emilie, whose pride alienated suitors. Emilie fell for Maximilien Longueville but was disillusioned upon learning of his shopkeeper status. Ultimately, she married the Comte de Kergarouet, haunted by her past choices.
