Doctor Monsieur Bianchon shares a chilling tale about La Grande Bretèche, a dilapidated house near Vendôme, hinting at its tragic past. Intrigued, he sneaks into the garden and learns from local notary Monsieur Regnault that the house belongs to the late Comtesse de Merret, whose will forbids entry or repairs for fifty years. Regnault recounts her reclusive life after her husband’s death and her intention to leave her estate to the hospital with strict conditions. Meanwhile, a woman discusses the mysterious disappearance of Count Férédia, linking him to Madame de Merret's death. As she seeks the truth from the maid Rosalie, the story reveals the emotional turmoil within La Grande Bretèche.
