**The Message**  
*By Honoré De Balzac*  
*Translated by Ellen Marriage*  

In this narrative, the author expresses a desire to share a poignant story that evokes fear and love among young lovers, compelling them to seek solace in each other. The tale is rooted in personal experience, blending reality with poetic potential.

In 1819, while traveling from Paris to Moulins, the narrator finds himself on the outside of a coach due to financial constraints. He soon strikes up a friendship with a fellow traveler, a young man of better means, and they bond over discussions of love and women, particularly those of a certain age—between thirty-five and forty. Their conversation is filled with youthful exuberance and romantic ideals, leading them to share intimate details about their respective mistresses, both of whom are older than they are.

As they exchange stories of their romantic escapades, the young men reveal their admiration for their mistresses, who are charming and devoted, despite being married. Their camaraderie deepens as they recount their adventures, including the risks they took to be with their lovers. However, their journey takes a tragic turn when the coach overturns, resulting in the death of the narrator's companion.

In his final moments, the young man, crushed beneath the coach, entrusts the narrator with a solemn task: to inform his mistress of his death. He provides the narrator with a key that will grant him access to his home and the letters he wishes to return to her. The young man dies, leaving the narrator with the heavy burden of delivering the news.

Upon reaching La Charité, the narrator fulfills his friend's last wishes, encountering grief from the household staff but focusing on the sorrow awaiting the countess. With limited funds, he decides to walk the remaining distance to the Countess de Montpersan's chateau, driven by a sense of urgency to deliver the tragic news.

As he approaches the chateau, he grapples with the emotional weight of his task, realizing the cruelty of being the bearer of such devastating news. Upon arrival, he finds the chateau open and bustling, but struggles to locate the countess. A young girl assists him, leading him through the park to find her mother.

This story captures the complexities of love, loss, and the burdens of communication, illustrating how a simple journey can transform into a profound encounter with fate. The narrator's reflections on youth, romance, and the inevitability of tragedy resonate throughout, emphasizing the delicate balance between joy and sorrow in human relationships.

I confess that I took great care to present myself well before meeting Juliette and her husband, M. le Comte and Mme. la Comtesse de Montpersan. I wanted to avoid being mistaken for a local rate collector, and as I reflect on that moment, I find it amusing.

As I composed myself, I spotted Juliette with her husband. She seemed slightly hurried, and upon noticing me, her expression revealed disappointment. I struggled to recall the elegant phrases I had prepared. This hesitation allowed her husband to approach, and I awkwardly inquired if they were indeed the Count and Countess. Observing them closely, I noted the husband’s pompous demeanor and shabby appearance, which contrasted sharply with the Countess's delicate beauty and grace. She was petite, charming, and exuded youthfulness, evoking a strong desire within me.

Realizing the situation, I decided to tactfully inform the Count of my purpose. I discreetly mentioned the death of my traveling companion, which visibly affected him. He expressed concern for his wife, and I assured him that I would not disclose the message I carried until he was informed. This earned me his trust, and we returned to the house where I was invited to dinner.

As we approached, Juliette cast a curious glance at us, revealing her mixed feelings about my unexpected presence. I felt a pang of sympathy for her, recalling her beauty in the sunlight. I then revealed that I had come on behalf of someone who called her Juliette, which startled her. When I mentioned her companion's illness, she grew anxious, fearing for his life. Her desperate plea for the truth struck me deeply, and I could only nod in response.

Just then, the Count called for dinner, and I followed him inside. The meal was lavish, but the Countess was notably absent. As we dined, a maid reported that she could not be found, prompting concern. The canon and I searched the grounds, calling for her, but to no avail. Eventually, we discovered her hidden in a hayloft, sobbing in despair. With the maid's help, we brought her to her room, instructing that she not be disturbed.

Upon returning to the dining room, I found the Count surprisingly unfazed, having consumed most of the meal. His indifference stemmed from a severe dietary restriction due to a medical condition, which explained his lack of concern for his wife’s absence. The evening was somber, filled with unanswered questions about Juliette's distress.

Later that night, I was awakened by the Countess, who appeared at my bedside, pale and shaken. She sought confirmation of the tragic news, and I recounted the accident, careful to spare her the most harrowing details. She listened intently, her grief palpable. When I revealed her companion's fears and the message he entrusted to me, her composure faltered, and she expressed regret for having burned his letters, feeling she had lost everything.

In a moment of connection, I handed her a lock of his hair, a tangible reminder of their bond. Her gratitude was overwhelming, and she wished me happiness in love, before retreating with her precious token.

The following day felt surreal as I reflected on the previous night. I spent time with Juliette, witnessing her noble spirit and selflessness. In the evening, the Count accompanied me partway to Moulins, asking me to deliver a sum of money to a contact in Paris. I willingly accepted, unaware of the thoughtful gesture behind her request. 

This experience deepened my understanding of love and sacrifice, leaving me with a poignant story to share with a woman who cherished me, reminding me of the fragility of life and the bonds we form.
