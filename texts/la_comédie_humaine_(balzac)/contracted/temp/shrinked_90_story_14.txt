**A Daughter of Eve**  
*By Honoré De Balzac*  

**Dedication**  
To Madame la Comtesse Bolognini, whose conversation brought joy to a traveler reminiscing about Paris. Your daughter Eugenie embodies true womanhood, unlike the Eugenie in this story, who faced a rigid upbringing. 

---

**Summary**  
In Paris, sisters Madame du Tillet and Madame de Vandenesse struggle with their loveless marriages, shaped by strict upbringing. Madame de Vandenesse confides her dissatisfaction, while both reflect on their lost innocence. Comte Felix de Vandenesse tries to nurture his wife, but monotony creeps in. Raoul Nathan, a rising literary figure, becomes entangled with Madame de Vandenesse, complicating her feelings. As their secret romance deepens, societal pressures mount. Nathan's despair leads him to contemplate suicide, prompting the countess to act. Their love faces challenges from her husband and societal expectations, culminating in difficult choices that shape their futures. Balzac's characters illustrate the complexities of love and ambition in 19th-century France.
