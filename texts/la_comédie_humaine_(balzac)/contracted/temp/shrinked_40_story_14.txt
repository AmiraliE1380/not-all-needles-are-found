DEDICATION

To Madame la Comtesse Bolognini.

Madame, recalling the pleasure of your conversation in Milan, I dedicate this work to you, hoping you will shelter it with your name as you once protected a beloved writer’s tales. You have a daughter, Eugénie, whose beauty and wit promise a happy childhood unlike the rigid upbringing of the Eugénie here. Though called fickle, I remain Italian in constancy and memory; writing “Eugénie” revives the warmth of Milan and makes you a distant, treasured masterpiece.

Your devoted servant,
De Balzac.

A DAUGHTER OF EVE

CHAPTER I. THE TWO MARIES

In a sumptuous blue-velvet boudoir on rue Neuve-des-Mathurins, two young women—Madame du Tillet and her sister Madame Félix de Vandenesse—weep by the fire. The room belongs to Ferdinand du Tillet, a wealthy banker who has bought social standing but lacks aristocratic ease. Reared by a strict, pious mother and married without youthful freedom, they entered matrimony innocent of passion; marriage, imagined as escape, reveals unforeseen realities.

CHAPTER II. A CONFIDENCE BETWEEN SISTERS

Madame de Vandenesse confides marital despair: a husband indifferent to her inner life. Madame du Tillet, equally helpless, describes similar cold domesticity. Both crave passion and excitement suppressed by society and duty, and seek consolation in one another.

CHAPTER III. THE HISTORY OF A FORTUNATE WOMAN

Félix de Vandenesse, of noble lineage, married hoping to revive an idealized love. The duties and constraints of his class cool the relationship; though kind, he cannot supply the romantic intensity his wife desires. Monotony awakens in her a longing for deeper emotional fulfillment and thoughts of love beyond marriage.

CHAPTER IV. A CELEBRATED MAN

Raoul Nathan, an advancing literary figure with talent, audacity, and caustic wit, attracts Madame de Vandenesse at Madame de Montcornet’s salon. Their exchanges hint at unspoken attraction. Nathan, juggling career strife and private troubles, sees her as a possible muse, though social decorum forbids an open liaison.

CHAPTER V. FLORINE

Florine, a gifted actress and Nathan’s longtime companion, balances affection with practicality: she sustains his spirits and career while depending on him for protection. As Nathan’s admiration for Madame de Vandenesse grows, Florine feels the shift and grows jealous.

CHAPTER VI. ROMANTIC LOVE

Nathan and Madame de Vandenesse bond over art and literature; their meetings grow intimate, bringing exhilaration and peril. She struggles with guilt and desire, torn between loyalty and the lure of a passionate life, aware of the social consequences.

CHAPTER VII. SUICIDE

Crushed by setbacks and despair, Nathan contemplates suicide. A chance meeting with Madame de Vandenesse revives his will to live; seeing his desperation, she resolves to protect and encourage him.

CHAPTER VIII. A LOVER SAVED AND LOST

After his crisis, she becomes more involved, balancing protection with her own feelings. Their secret relationship becomes both strength and turmoil as social risk and Florine’s jealousy complicate matters. Nathan, improving in fortune, is torn between Florine’s practical devotion and the countess’s inspiring influence.

CHAPTER IX. THE HUSBAND’S TRIUMPH

Félix, sensing his wife’s attachment, moves to preserve their marriage. Determined to uncover the truth and shield her from scandal, he acts to defend her honor and their union. Love, ambition, loyalty, and expectation remain entangled, leading to a dramatic clash between desire and duty.
