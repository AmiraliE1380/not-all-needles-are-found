In October 1800, Bartolomeo di Piombo, after killing the Portas for his son's death, pledged loyalty to the Bonaparte family and moved to Paris. Fifteen years later, Ginevra Piombo, ostracized yet dedicated to her art, faced challenges from Amelie but connected with Luigi, a hidden officer. Despite her father's anger over her love for Luigi, Ginevra remained steadfast, asserting his innocence. After being cast out by her father, she rented a room near Luigi. On their wedding day, they felt family absence, and financial struggles worsened after their son Bartolomeo's birth. Luigi sold himself as a military substitute, only to return home to find Ginevra and their child dead, leaving him devastated.
