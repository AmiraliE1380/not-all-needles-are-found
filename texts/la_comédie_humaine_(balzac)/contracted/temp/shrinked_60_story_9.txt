The Marquise de Listomere embodies the Restoration's mix of religious devotion and elegance. Married to the unremarkable Marquis, she upholds a virtuous image for his political gain. After dancing with Eugène de Rastignac, who chose not to pursue her, she was intrigued by his letter but ultimately burned it, surprising her maid. Disappointed by his absence at a party, she later learned he mistakenly sent her a love letter meant for another. Their conversation revealed her skepticism about his intentions, leading her to withdraw from society under the guise of illness, reflecting the complexities of romantic misunderstandings.
