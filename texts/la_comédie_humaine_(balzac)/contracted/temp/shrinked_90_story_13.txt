**LETTERS OF TWO BRIDES**  
**By Honoré de Balzac**  

**DEDICATION**  
To George Sand, whose friendship has enriched my literary journey.

**LETTERS OF TWO BRIDES**

**FIRST PART**

**I. LOUISE DE CHAULIEU TO RENEE DE MAUCOMBE. PARIS, September.**  
Dear Renee, I am free! My aunt released me due to health concerns after your departure. In Paris, I feel our souls are intertwined. My inheritance is intact, but my family home remains unclaimed, leaving me anxious about my future. I long for our conversations.

**October 19th.**  
I met my mother, who treated me as an equal, and my father was affectionate. I’m preparing for my society debut with a generous allowance and a governess, Miss Griffith.

**II. THE SAME TO THE SAME November 25th.**  
I’m settling into my new life, acquiring clothes for my debut. Miss Griffith is a good companion, while my family is preoccupied with their lives. I haven’t explored Paris yet but look forward to it.

**III. THE SAME TO THE SAME December.**  
I’m ready for society! I’ve prepared well and hope to charm those around me. Yet, I feel regret for my convent past and notice my family’s distance as I navigate this new world. I’m eager to understand the allure of society and my family’s mysteries.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
On December 15th, I drove through Paris, feeling unnoticed despite my efforts. My mother received attention while I felt overshadowed at a ball. Men lacked elegance, and one intellectual seemed self-absorbed. I resolved to enjoy dancing despite societal indifference, but my mother’s advice on discretion left me conflicted about expressing joy.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Your letter moved me as I reflect on our different lives. While you embrace Paris, I find peace at the Castle of Maucombe. I met Louis de l'Estorade, my intended, during a family dinner. Despite his humble background, I agreed to marry him, hoping to improve his estate. I envision a simple life filled with nature and family, free from past turmoil. Please share your Parisian adventures with me, as I will be there in spirit. P.S. I have 1,000 livres for you to buy lovely things for me.
################################################

**Summary of Correspondence Between Don Felipe Henarez and Don Fernand Paris, and Letters from Louise de Chaulieu to Renee de Maucombe**

Don Felipe reassures his brother Fernand of his safety amid Spain's turmoil, reflecting on their family's tragic past. He laments his estates' decline and urges Fernand to claim his titles, encouraging his union with Marie Heredia. In France, Felipe finds solace in simplicity, expressing love for Spain and hope for Fernand and Marie. Meanwhile, Louise warns Renee against hasty marriage, critiques societal superficiality, and shares her interest in the dignified Don Henarez, navigating her desire for genuine connection amidst societal expectations.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Dear child, I embrace my responsibilities in family life, focusing on motherhood and home.

############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Mlle. de Chaulieu expresses distress over her friend Renee's upcoming marriage, fearing it will lead to disillusionment. She becomes intrigued by M. Henarez, a Spanish teacher, and reflects on love outside marriage. Mme. de L'Estorade warns her against him, sharing her own marital struggles. Mlle. de Chaulieu discusses family obligations with her father and her growing feelings for M. Henarez. She critiques Mme. de L'Estorade's pragmatic views on love, emphasizing passion. Their correspondence reveals contrasting perspectives on love, marriage, and personal fulfillment.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
In her letter to Mme. de L'Estorade, Louise reflects on love and ambition, questioning the balance between virtue and manipulation. She confesses her love for Felipe, feeling both drawn to and restrained by him. After receiving a heartfelt response from Felipe, she experiences joy and hope for their future. Despite the challenges, she longs for him to express his feelings more boldly. She also wonders about her friend's happiness and independence.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Renee critiques marriage for forcing women into subservience, lamenting her loss of identity and longing for love beyond societal norms. Meanwhile, Louise grapples with her desire for Felipe, planning a secret meeting to explore their feelings while reflecting on love's complexities and societal constraints. Both women seek fulfillment amid societal expectations.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Louise tells Felipe that if he doesn't find Racine's _Berenice_ moving, they should part ways. She criticizes his changed demeanor and expresses mistrust, emphasizing her need for genuine love. Louise warns that any sign of diminished passion would lead to despair, and she desires him to embody noble qualities. Despite her disappointment, she still admires him and hopes for a deeper connection.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Felipe expresses remorse and devotion to Louise, appreciating her jealousy as a sign of love. He longs for emotional closeness and fears losing her affection. Louise, testing Felipe's love, admires his loyalty and contemplates societal pressures regarding marriage. She shares her love for him and their engagement, while Renee warns of the challenges of married life. Themes of love, loyalty, and societal expectations are explored throughout their correspondence.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Dear friend, it has been eight months since our marriage, and your silence is inexcusable. Felipe and I have settled into a blissful life at Chantepleurs, where I’ve discovered the profound joy of love. I admire your strength, Renee, and hope you find happiness with Louis. Encourage him to pursue a political career; it could lead to a new beginning for us both.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Dear Louise, your letter brought me joy and envy. As I reflect on my pregnancy, I feel the stirrings of motherhood, transforming my view of sacrifice into strength. While you experience love with Felipe, my love grows for my future children. I grapple with strange cravings, yet I remain emotionally detached from this journey. I urge you to stay humble in your happiness, as joy can attract misfortune. Despite my concerns, I cherish our friendship. God bless you!
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Renee has given birth to a healthy boy, Armand-Louis, whom we hope you will baptize.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
In January 1826, Louise de Macumer eagerly anticipates visiting her friend Renee de l'Estorade and becoming the godmother to her child. Despite her childlessness, Louise finds joy in her marriage to Felipe, though she contemplates the complexities of motherhood. Renee, now a mother, shares her emotional journey and the deep bond with her son, Armand. She reflects on the transformative nature of motherhood, urging Louise to embrace her future role. Their correspondence highlights their contrasting yet complementary experiences of love and motherhood.

**Summary of Correspondence Between Mme. de Macumer and Mme. de L'Estorade (March - July 1826)**

Mme. de Macumer expresses guilt for her delayed response and reflects on her busy social life in Paris, feeling overwhelmed yet influenced by society. She longs for genuine friendship and plans to visit Mme. de L'Estorade. In her letters, she reveals jealousy over Felipe's attention to Mme. de L'Estorade, who advises her to embrace a supportive role in her marriage for true happiness. Mme. de L'Estorade encourages her to return to Paris for growth and balance in their relationships.

**Summary of Correspondence Between the Baronne de Macumer and the Vicomtesse de L'Estorade**

Baronne de Macumer expresses her hurt from Vicomtesse de L'Estorade's letter but finds solace in Felipe's love. Despite a fainting episode, she reassures her friend of their bond. The Vicomtesse shares her family’s struggles and warns against love, reflecting on her husband’s political ambitions. She recounts a harrowing experience with her sick child, revealing her fears. The Baronne updates the Vicomtesse on Louis's promotion and her plans to retreat to Chantepleurs, inviting her to visit without jealousy, as Felipe's love for her has grown.

**Summary of Correspondence Between Mme. de Macumer and the Comtesse de L'Estorade**

Mme. de Macumer expresses deep sorrow over her childlessness, longing for motherhood and feeling desolate. She plans to consult a doctor and yearns for her friend’s company. In contrast, the Comtesse de L'Estorade shares her busy life with children, emphasizing the joys and challenges of motherhood. Mme. de Macumer later mourns Felipe's death, haunted by memories and guilt, and invites her friend for comfort during her grief.

**Summary of the Letter from the Baronne de Macumer to the Comtesse de l'Estorade (October 15, 1833)**

Dear Renee,

I sold my properties and now have about twelve hundred thousand francs, investing a million for a better income. I’m marrying Marie Gaston soon, a poet with debts I’ve cleared. Despite our age difference, I’m confident in our love. I worry about aging and potential loss, asking you to care for my children if needed. Our marriage will be private at a secluded property. I’ll invest in my appearance to nurture our romance. Address future letters to Madame Gaston, Poste Restante, Versailles. I will miss you dearly.
################################################

**Summary of Correspondence Between Marie Gaston and Daniel D'Arthez, Mme. de L'Estorade and Mme. de Macumer, and the Comtesse de L'Estorade to Mme. Marie Gaston**

Marie Gaston writes to Daniel D'Arthez, requesting him and Joseph Bridau as witnesses for his marriage. He expresses love for his future wife, who prefers seclusion, and shares his feelings of shame over their wealth disparity. Mme. de L'Estorade warns Mme. de Macumer against a solitary life post-marriage, fearing it may lead to unhappiness. The Comtesse de L'Estorade, concerned about Louise's silence, updates her on family matters and expresses worries about her children's futures, particularly Nais's potential marriage.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Dear Renee, 

After two years, I share my happiness: Gaston and I are deeply in love, enjoying our simple life together. He is kind and graceful, and I cherish our home and shared moments. Yet, I struggle with jealousy and the desire for children as I approach thirty. I hope we stay in touch and I look forward to hearing about your life.

With love,  
Your friend.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
**Summary of Correspondence Between Mme. de L'Estorade and Mme. Gaston**

Mme. de L'Estorade advises Mme. Gaston to mature her view of love, emphasizing that true love requires sacrifices and responsibilities in marriage. She urges her to return to Paris, warning against the dangers of pursuing fleeting passions. Mme. Gaston, meanwhile, grapples with jealousy and suspicion regarding Gaston's secretive behavior, fearing he is involved with another woman. After discovering he supports his late brother's family, Mme. de L'Estorade reassures her that Gaston's actions are rooted in kindness, not betrayal. Mme. Gaston, however, feels overwhelmed by despair and longs for support.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
In her August 7th letter, the Comtesse de L'Estorade explains her choice to stay with her dying friend, Louise, instead of traveling to Provence. Louise, aware of her impending death from consumption, reflects on love and societal expectations. Despite her illness, she maintains charm and humor. The Comtesse witnesses Louise's final moments on August 26th, feeling a profound loss and longing for her children, highlighting the bittersweet nature of love and relationships.
