**The Purse**  
*By Honoré De Balzac*  
*Translated by Clara Bell*

**To Sofka**

In the twilight hour, a young painter named Hippolyte Schinner, lost in contemplation of his nearly finished artwork, accidentally falls and loses consciousness. He is rescued by two women, Madame Leseigneur and her daughter Adelaide, who care for him until he regains his senses. The painter is struck by Adelaide's beauty, which embodies the ideal he has long sought in art.

Hippolyte, a talented artist recently recognized for his work, has moved into a modest studio in a less affluent part of Paris. He is devoted to his mother, who raised him through hardship, and now seeks to provide her with a better life. Despite his growing fame, he remains reserved and private, reluctant to share his personal life.

After his accident, Hippolyte becomes increasingly enamored with Adelaide, imagining a connection between their lives. He visits the women frequently, drawn to their refined manners and the mystery surrounding their circumstances. The old lady and her daughter live in a modest apartment, and while they appear genteel, hints of poverty linger in their surroundings.

As their acquaintance deepens, Hippolyte offers to paint a portrait of Madame Leseigneur, which she accepts with grace. The painter's feelings for Adelaide grow stronger, and their interactions become more intimate. However, he is troubled by suspicions regarding their financial situation, particularly after overhearing gossip about them from friends.

One evening, after a game of cards, Hippolyte realizes his purse, containing fifteen gold louis, is missing. He suspects Adelaide and her mother of theft, which devastates him. Despite his heartbreak, he cannot fully abandon his feelings for Adelaide, believing in her inherent goodness.

Days pass, and Hippolyte struggles with his emotions, torn between love and betrayal. He decides to confront his feelings and visits the Leseigneurs again, only to find Adelaide looking pale and distressed. Their connection rekindles, and he feels guilty for doubting her integrity.

During a card game, Adelaide discreetly returns his purse, revealing her thoughtfulness and affection. Overwhelmed with joy and remorse for his earlier suspicions, Hippolyte embraces her and expresses his desire to marry her. Their love blossoms, and the barriers of class and circumstance begin to dissolve.

As the story unfolds, the arrival of Madame Schinner, Hippolyte's mother, and the vice-admiral, a family friend, brings resolution. The truth about the Leseigneurs' situation is revealed, dispelling the rumors that had clouded Hippolyte's judgment. The evening culminates in a sense of unity and familial bonds, as the characters come together, celebrating love and understanding amidst the complexities of life.

**Paris, May 1832**
