**La Grenadière** by Honoré de Balzac, translated by Ellen Marriage, depicts a charming house on the Loire River, surrounded by gardens and vineyards. The property includes terraced gardens and a rustic house, reflecting its history as a retreat for townsfolk.

In the spring of the Restoration, Mme. Willemsens arrived at La Grenadière with her housekeeper and two children, seeking solitude. Known as Augusta Willemsens, Countess of Brandon, her mysterious life intrigued locals. She focused on nurturing her children, Louis-Gaston and Marie-Gaston, balancing lessons and play while instilling resilience.

As her health declined, the atmosphere grew somber, yet Louis excelled in his studies, motivated by his mother’s encouragement. In October, she shared tender moments with her children before becoming bedridden. On her deathbed, she instructed Louis to care for Marie and pursue a career at sea.

After her passing, Louis embraced his responsibilities, determined to honor his mother’s memory and embody her resilience.
