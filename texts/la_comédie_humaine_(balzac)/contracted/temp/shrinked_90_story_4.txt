In October 1800, Bartolomeo di Piombo, a foreigner, confronts Napoleon after avenging his son's death by killing the Portas. Despite his desire for support, Napoleon offers him a chance to live in Paris, away from vendetta culture. Bartolomeo accepts, pledging loyalty to the Bonaparte family.

**Summary:**

Servin, a respected artist, opened a studio for wealthy young girls to learn painting, fostering a reputation for integrity. Among the students was Ginevra Piombo, a talented Bonapartist ostracized by royalist peers, particularly Amelie Thirion. Tensions rose as Amelie attempted to undermine Ginevra, who remained focused on her art and a hidden officer's safety. Servin recognized Ginevra's talent, intensifying the rivalry, while she resolved to protect the officer amidst the growing animosity.

In a dim garret, painter Servin introduces Ginevra to Luigi, a wounded officer shaken by Labedoyere's death sentence. Ginevra offers her savings to help him, sparking a deep connection. Their friendship grows as they share stories and devise secret communications, despite rumors about their relationship. Ginevra's parents worry about her late return, highlighting themes of loyalty, love, and societal judgment.

In Chapter IV, Ginevra reveals her love for Luigi Porta, a man without fortune and linked to her family's vendetta. Her father, Piombo, reacts with disbelief and anger, insisting she should love no one but him. Despite his threats and attempts to control her, Ginevra remains resolute, declaring her intention to marry Luigi. After being cast out by her father, she finds solace in Luigi's love, vowing to seek happiness together.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
After Ginevra was expelled from her father's house, she sought refuge with Madame Servin, who refused to help. Hurt, Ginevra rented a room near Luigi, who supported her. On their wedding day, lacking family presence, they married in a somber ceremony. Despite initial struggles, they found happiness in their love and work, culminating in Ginevra's portrait for Luigi, symbolizing their commitment.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
In early 1819, Ginevra faced pressure to create original art amid fierce competition. Despite her efforts in portraiture and Luigi's struggles with declining income, their financial situation worsened. After the birth of their son, Bartolomeo, they sold possessions to survive. Ginevra's health declined, and after losing their child, she died in Luigi's arms. Their estranged parents, upon learning of their tragedy, were devastated by the loss and consequences of their separation.
