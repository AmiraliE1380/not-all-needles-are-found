In the enchanting twilight, artist Hippolyte Schinner falls from his ladder while finishing a painting and loses consciousness. He awakens to the care of Adelaide and her mother, captivated by Adelaide's beauty. Recently moved into a modest Paris studio, Hippolyte has gained some fame and financial stability but remains humble and devoted to his mother.

After recovering, he feels a connection to Adelaide and visits their apartment, which reflects both elegance and poverty. Mademoiselle Leseigneur opens the door, and Hippolyte is struck by the contrast between their living conditions and Adelaide's beauty. Inside, the warm yet weary atmosphere deepens their bond when Madame Leseigneur accepts his offer to paint her portrait. She shares her past, including her late husband, a naval officer who died in battle, and as they discuss challenges, Hippolyte feels a strong desire to help them.

Adelaide lightens the mood, but their conversation is interrupted by two men, one adding intrigue. Hippolyte admires Adelaide more, feeling hope and uncertainty about their social differences. However, suspicions about Madame de Rouville's financial situation cloud his happiness, especially after discovering his missing purse. This incident shatters his idealized view, prompting doubts about their integrity. Despite heartbreak, he remains drawn to Adelaide. A chance encounter reveals rumors complicating his feelings, leading him to confront his suspicions. Their reunion is bittersweet, but Adelaide discreetly replaces his lost purse, rekindling his hope.

The arrival of the vice-admiral, an old friend of Madame de Rouville, defends her against rumors, shifting the atmosphere. The story culminates in Hippolyte’s heartfelt proposal to Adelaide, met with surprise and joy, concluding with a sense of unity among the characters while leaving shadows of doubt.
