For effusive souls, twilight — that soft, silent hour between day and night — invites contemplation and reflection on art. During such an hour a young painter, absorbed in finishing a large canvas, falls from his ladder, strikes his head, and loses consciousness.

He wakes to the gentle voice of a young woman and her mother, who had rushed to his aid after hearing the fall. As he regains his senses he is struck by the girl's beauty, matching the ideal of feminine grace he has long imagined. The older woman, resembling a refined marquise, tends him kindly and advises on his recovery. The painter thanks them, but the two women, naturally refined, depart without pressing for further acquaintance.

After they leave, the porter’s wife tells him the visitors are Mademoiselle Leseigneur and her mother, long-time residents of the building. The painter, Hippolyte Schinner, has recently moved into the attic studio, having won some recognition. He is determined to provide better for his mother, who sacrificed much for him. Despite his growing success he remains reserved and does not easily share his private life.

During his convalescence Hippolyte keeps returning in thought to the two women, especially the young girl's image, which lingers. When he is well enough to return to his studio he is eager to visit them, feeling a bond with their situation; he imagines they share a kind of misfortune that draws him to them.

At their modest home he notes the signs of poverty but feels no contempt, only empathy. Mademoiselle Leseigneur greets him politely, and he is struck by the contrast between their humble surroundings and the elegance they display. The conversation begins stiffly but soon turns to art, allowing Hippolyte to speak freely about his thoughts and feelings. As they talk, the young woman, Adelaide, steps out briefly and returns with candles to light the room. The atmosphere shifts when an unexpected visitor arrives — a man who seems acquainted with Adelaide. His presence stirs jealousy in Hippolyte, who wants to know the nature of their relationship.

The newcomer is an older gentleman in a coat whose style recalls a bygone era; he carries an air of confidence that mixes nobility with libertinism, suggesting a complicated past. The exchange between him and the women reveals layers of their lives, leaving Hippolyte to ponder the dynamics at play and the histories hinted at by their manner and circumstances.

Throughout the evening Hippolyte struggles with his feelings for Adelaide — admiration tangled with uncertainty. The contrast in their social standings weighs on him, yet he is drawn to her spirit and grace. As night unfolds he becomes increasingly conscious of the delicate balance between their worlds and of the slender hope for connection amid the shadows of their respective circumstances.

A remarkable figure stood beside the “Louis XIV’s light infantry” specimen — Bonapartists’ nickname for those remnants of the Monarchy. A lean, aged man, he was like a shadow of the first: a worn coat, duller hair powder, a feebler intellect. He seemed the faded reflection Rivarol’s quip on Champcenetz suggested.

This silent old man puzzled the painter, the Chevalier. He neither spoke nor was spoken to; his relation to the gallant man was unclear—friend, relative, or mere companion? His presence invited curiosity but offered no answers, especially about any past connection to the gallant’s deceased wife.

The least dilapidated of the group approached Madame de Rouville, kissed her hand, and sat beside her. The other man bowed and took a seat nearby. Adelaide leaned over the old gentleman’s chair, echoing a famous painting’s pose, though the gentleman’s familiarity seemed to irritate her.

“Are you sulky with me?” he asked, casting a shrewd glance at Schinner, the painter, as if to judge his standing.

Seeing Adelaide, pale and sad, he felt ashamed of his earlier suspicions. Their talk revealed mutual longing, and he realized his mistake. The evening turned sweet as they played cards, but his fears returned when he noticed Adelaide’s secretive glances at her mother.
