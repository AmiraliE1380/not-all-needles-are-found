Felix Gaudissart, a successful commercial traveler selling Parisian hats, shifts to life insurance and journalism after the 1830 political upheaval to meet the public's demand for new ideas. While training and writing, he falls for florist Jenny, vowing to support her. In Vouvray, he encounters skepticism from locals, including Monsieur Vernier and eccentric Margaritis. Their humorous duel challenge leads to reconciliation over breakfast, teaching Gaudissart humility and reflecting Balzac's themes of commerce and human nature. Characters from this story also appear in Balzac's Human Comedy.
