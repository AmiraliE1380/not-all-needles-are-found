Bianchon, a Parisian physician, trained under the troubled Desplein, who grappled with atheism and sought a legacy. Despite his poverty, Bianchon was a loyal student. After the 1830 Revolution, he learned of Desplein's past struggles with isolation and bitterness from competition in Paris, and their bond deepened as Desplein cared for his supportive neighbor, Bourgeat, until his death.
