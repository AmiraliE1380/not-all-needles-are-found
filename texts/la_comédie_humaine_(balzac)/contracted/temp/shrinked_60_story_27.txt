In 1828, Dr. Horace Bianchon warned Baron de Rastignac against pursuing the Marquise d’Espard, comparing her to a “one-eyed horse” and suggesting she was not as youthful as he thought. Despite concerns about her superficiality and potential debts, Rastignac remained attracted to her wealth and status. Bianchon agreed to introduce Rastignac to his uncle, Judge Jean-Jules Popinot, known for his integrity but often overlooked.

Popinot had shifted from a judge to a compassionate figure aiding the poor, despite his own financial struggles. The narrative then shifts to a case involving the Marquis d’Espard, whose wife sought a Commission in Lunacy for him. Popinot, skeptical yet aware of the need for investigation, meets Madame d’Espard, questioning her motives and planning to summon another key figure, Jeanrenaud, to explore the complexities of their situation.

The text explores the life of M. le Marquis d’Espard, a nobleman in a decaying Paris mansion, illustrating how minor events can have significant consequences. A small illness delayed a meeting with a judge, emphasizing the impact of trivial incidents.

D’Espard's home symbolizes the decline of French nobility. Chosen for its garden, it provided a peaceful atmosphere but led to misunderstandings with neighbors, who viewed him as eccentric and gossiped about his finances.

D’Espard and his wife instilled dignity in their sons. On a holiday, Popinot planned to question the Marquis, who learned of a petition for a Commission in Lunacy filed by his wife. Distressed by financial allegations, he revealed a family secret about the Jeanrenaud fortune, which he felt compelled to repay. 

Madame Jeanrenaud sought to return the money, and Popinot reassured her of its legality. After reflecting on the encounter, he was removed from the case due to a conflict of interest, prioritizing the Court's integrity. The narrative explores themes of honor, familial duty, and social standing as the Marquis confronts his family's troubled history while striving to restore their honor.
