The Marquise de Listomere embodies the Restoration's mix of religious devotion and worldly elegance. Married for seven years to the unremarkable Marquis, she upholds a virtuous image to support his political ambitions. Known as "dull weather," the marquis provides a stable home life that aligns with her values.

Despite her reserved nature, the Marquise commands respect and skillfully deflects flirtation, preserving her dignity. After dancing with the charming Eugène de Rastignac, whose views on women intrigued her, she chose not to pursue him further. The next morning, while writing letters, Rastignac reflected on her, interrupted by his friend Horace.

The Marquise received a letter from Rastignac, which she initially considered discarding but ultimately burned, surprising her maid. At a party, she anticipated Rastignac's arrival but felt disappointment when he did not come. Four days later, Rastignac realized he had mistakenly sent a love letter meant for another to her. When he visited, their conversation revealed tensions, with Madame de Listomere expressing skepticism about his intentions. She later withdrew from society, claiming illness, while the marquis masked her nervousness, highlighting the complexities of romantic misunderstandings.
