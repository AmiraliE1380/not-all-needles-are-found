The Marquise de Listomere embodies the Restoration's mix of religious devotion and worldly elegance. Married for seven years to the unremarkable Marquis de Listomere, she upholds a virtuous image to support his political ambitions. Known as "dull weather," the marquis provides a stable home life that aligns with her values.

Despite her reserved nature, the Marquise commands respect and skillfully deflects flirtatious advances, maintaining her dignity. Her beauty is subtle, revealing depth when she is animated. Recently, she danced with Eugène de Rastignac, a charming young man whose conversation about women's roles left a lasting impression. Although captivated by her, Rastignac chose not to pursue her further that evening.

The next morning, Rastignac reflected on the Marquise while writing letters, interrupted by his friend Horace discussing an expedition to Morea. Meanwhile, the Marquise received a letter from Rastignac, which intrigued her despite her initial impulse to discard it. This moment highlights her internal conflict between societal expectations and personal desires.

Deciding to forbid her porter from admitting Rastignac, she resolved to show disdain for his insolence. Although she considered keeping the love letter, she ultimately burned it, surprising her maid, Caroline. At a party hosted by the Marquis de Beauseant, she anticipated Rastignac's arrival but felt disappointment when he did not come, leading to a process of emotional crystallization.

Four days later, Rastignac learned he had mistakenly sent a love letter meant for another to Madame de Listomere. When he finally visited, the marquis invited him in, and Rastignac noticed the Marquise blush, flattering his vanity. Their conversation revealed underlying tensions, with Madame de Listomere expressing skepticism about his intentions.

Days later, she withdrew from society, claiming illness, while the marquis attributed her absence to stomach issues. In reality, she was experiencing a nervous attack, using it as an excuse to avoid social interactions, highlighting the complexities of romantic misunderstandings among the young.
