The Marquise de Listomere embodies the Restoration's mix of religious devotion and elegance. Married to the unremarkable Marquis, she upholds a virtuous image to support his political goals. Though reserved, she commands respect and reveals her beauty when animated. 

After dancing with Eugène de Rastignac, who chose not to pursue her, she received a letter from him that intrigued her, despite her initial urge to discard it. Torn between societal expectations and personal desires, she forbade his entry and burned the letter, surprising her maid. 

At a party, she felt disappointment when Rastignac did not arrive. Four days later, he realized he had mistakenly sent her a love letter meant for another. Their conversation revealed tensions, with the Marquise skeptical of his intentions. She later withdrew from society, claiming illness while actually experiencing a nervous attack, highlighting the complexities of romantic misunderstandings.
