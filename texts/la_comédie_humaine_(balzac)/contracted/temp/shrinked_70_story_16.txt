In spring 1822, M. le Baron Gaston de Nueil visited Bayeux to recover from overwork, feeling constrained by local elites and his family's reputation. He became fascinated with the ostracized Mme. de Beauseant, who initially hesitated to meet him but was eventually captivated by their connection. After a confession led to her disdain, Gaston wrote her a heartfelt letter. Despite her urging him to focus on his future due to their age difference, he followed her to Geneva for three years. 

After family tragedies, he returned to France, where they maintained a discreet relationship. However, societal pressures and the arrival of Mlle. de la Rodiere led Gaston to end things with the Vicomtesse. She destroyed his farewell letter, sensing the truth. Gaston married Mlle. de la Rodiere but became withdrawn, secretly communicating with the Vicomtesse. When his letters went unanswered, he succumbed to despair and took his own life, illustrating the emptiness of a loveless marriage.
