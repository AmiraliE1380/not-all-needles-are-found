In the enchanting twilight, artist Hippolyte Schinner, while finishing a painting, fell from his ladder and lost consciousness. He awoke to the soothing voices of Adelaide and her mother, leaving him captivated by Adelaide's beauty. Recently moved into a modest Paris studio, Hippolyte gained fame and financial stability through his art, remaining humble and devoted to his mother.

After recovering, he visited Adelaide and her mother, Mademoiselle Leseigneur, in their apartment, struck by the contrast between their humble living conditions and Adelaide's beauty. The warm atmosphere hinted at better days, and Madame Leseigneur welcomed him, leading to a deeper bond when he offered to paint her portrait. She shared her past, including her late husband, a naval officer who died in battle, and Hippolyte felt a strong desire to help them.

As he admired Adelaide, their conversation was interrupted by two men, one an older gentleman, adding intrigue. Despite his growing affection for Adelaide, Hippolyte worried about their financial situation, especially after discovering his missing purse containing fifteen louis d’or, which shattered his idealized view of them.

Despite heartbreak, he remained drawn to Adelaide, struggling with his feelings. A chance encounter revealed rumors about Madame de Rouville, prompting him to confront his suspicions. When he visited again, he found Adelaide distressed, and during a card game, she discreetly replaced his lost purse, rekindling his hope. The arrival of the vice-admiral, an old friend of Madame de Rouville, defended her against rumors, shifting the atmosphere. The story culminates in Hippolyte’s heartfelt proposal to Adelaide, met with joy, as they embrace their intertwined fates amidst lingering doubts.
