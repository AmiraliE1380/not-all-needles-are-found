In twilight, artist Hippolyte Schinner falls from a ladder and loses consciousness, awakening to the care of Adelaide and her mother, which leaves a lasting impression on him. Now living in a modest Paris studio, he has gained some fame but remains humble and devoted to his mother. After recovering, he visits Adelaide's apartment, struck by their humble conditions and her beauty. 

Madame Leseigneur welcomes him, and he offers to paint her portrait, deepening their bond as she shares her past. Despite challenges, he feels compelled to help them. However, he grows suspicious of Madame de Rouville's finances after discovering his missing purse, which complicates his feelings for Adelaide. A chance encounter reveals rumors about Madame de Rouville, but when Adelaide discreetly replaces his lost purse, hope is rekindled. The arrival of the vice-admiral defends Madame de Rouville, shifting the atmosphere. The story culminates in Hippolyte’s heartfelt proposal to Adelaide, uniting their fates while leaving shadows of doubt.
