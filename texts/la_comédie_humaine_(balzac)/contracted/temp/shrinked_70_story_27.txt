In 1828, Dr. Horace Bianchon cautioned Baron de Rastignac about pursuing the Marquise d’Espard, highlighting her superficiality and debts. Despite this, Rastignac was drawn to her wealth. Bianchon introduced him to Judge Jean-Jules Popinot, a compassionate figure aiding the poor despite his own struggles. The story shifts to the Marquis d’Espard, whose wife sought a Commission in Lunacy for him. Popinot, skeptical of her motives, planned to investigate further.

The Marquis's decaying mansion symbolizes the decline of French nobility, with minor events significantly impacting lives. Distressed by financial allegations, the Marquis revealed a family secret regarding the Jeanrenaud fortune. After Madame Jeanrenaud sought to return the money, Popinot reassured her of its legality but was later removed from the case due to a conflict of interest. The narrative explores themes of honor, duty, and social standing as the Marquis confronts his family's troubled history.
