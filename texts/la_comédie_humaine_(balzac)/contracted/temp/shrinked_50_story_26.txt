Bianchon, a Parisian physician, trained under the troubled Desplein, who grappled with atheism and legacy. Despite his poverty, Bianchon was a loyal and promising student. Intrigued by Desplein's church visit, he discovered Desplein had founded the service, contradicting his beliefs. After the 1830 Revolution, Bianchon learned of Desplein's past struggles with poverty and isolation, shaped by the selfishness he observed in Paris. Desplein's neighbor, Bourgeat, supported him financially, and their bond deepened as Desplein cared for Bourgeat until his death, honoring his faith with masses.
