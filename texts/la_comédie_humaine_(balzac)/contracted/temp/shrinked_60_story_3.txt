In twilight, artist Hippolyte Schinner falls from a ladder and loses consciousness, awakening to the care of Adelaide and her mother, which leaves a lasting impression. Recently moved to a modest Paris studio, he has gained some fame but remains humble. After recovering, he visits Adelaide's apartment, struck by their humble conditions. Madame Leseigneur welcomes him, and they bond over a portrait session, revealing complexities of love and aspiration. 

Hippolyte grows suspicious of Madame de Rouville's finances after discovering his missing purse, prompting doubts about their integrity. Despite this, he remains drawn to Adelaide. A chance encounter reveals rumors about Madame de Rouville, complicating his feelings. When he visits again, Adelaide discreetly replaces his lost purse, rekindling hope. The arrival of the vice-admiral defends Madame de Rouville, shifting the atmosphere. The story culminates in Hippolyte’s heartfelt proposal to Adelaide, uniting their fates while leaving behind shadows of doubt.
