**Study of a Woman**  
*By Honoré De Balzac*  
*Translated by Katharine Prescott Wormeley*

**Dedication**  
To the Marquis Jean-Charles di Negro.

---

The Marquise de Listomere represents the spirit of the Restoration, blending religious devotion with worldly elegance. Married for seven years to the unremarkable Marquis de Listomere, she maintains a virtuous facade, possibly to bolster her husband's political ambitions. Known as "dull weather," the marquis ensures a stable home life that aligns with the Marquise's principles.

Despite her reserved demeanor, the Marquise commands respect and skillfully deflects the advances of flirtatious men, preserving her dignity. She engages in conversations with agreeable men without attracting scandal, a challenging feat in her social circle. Her beauty is understated; her striking features shine when animated, revealing her depth of character.

Recently, she danced with Eugène de Rastignac, a clever young man embodying charm and complexity. Their conversation, which began with light topics and shifted to women's roles, left a lasting impression. Rastignac, ambitious yet playful, found himself captivated by the Marquise but chose not to pursue her further that evening.

The next morning, Rastignac awoke contemplatively, reflecting on the Marquise while writing letters. Lost in thoughts of her, he was interrupted by his friend Horace, and they discussed various topics, including a potential expedition to Morea, though the conversation was ultimately unremarkable.

Meanwhile, the Marquise received a letter from Rastignac while preparing for the day. The letter's romantic declarations prompted her to read on despite her initial impulse to discard it. As she absorbed its contents, she felt a mix of fatigue and intrigue, leading her to ponder Rastignac's bold approach.

This moment reflects the complexities of her situation—caught between societal expectations and her desires. The letter challenges her perceptions of propriety and ignites curiosity about Rastignac's intentions, setting the stage for a deeper exploration of her character and the societal dynamics at play.

Madame de Listomere decided to forbid her porter from admitting Monsieur de Rastignac and resolved to show him disdain due to his insolence. Initially considering keeping a love letter she received, she ultimately burned it, surprising her housemaid, Caroline.

At a party hosted by the Marquis de Beauseant, Madame de Listomere anticipated Rastignac's arrival. By 2 AM, realizing he would not come, her thoughts underwent a process Stendhal termed "crystallization."

Four days later, Rastignac scolded his valet, Joseph, for misplacing letters. Joseph revealed one was for Madame de Listomere, prompting Rastignac to realize he had mistakenly sent her a love letter not meant for her. This blunder led him to reflect on his mistakes, particularly not visiting her soon after, allowing her to dwell on the incident.

When Rastignac finally visited, the porter informed him that Madame de Listomere was out, but the marquis invited him in. Upon entering, Rastignac noticed Madame de Listomere blush at his presence, which flattered his vanity. The marquis read the newspaper while Madame de Listomere maintained her composure despite the awkwardness.

Rastignac attempted to explain the misunderstanding about the letter, but Madame de Listomere, feigning offense, seemed amused. She insisted on forgetting the incident, but Rastignac clarified that no offense had occurred. Their conversation revealed underlying tensions, with Madame de Listomere expressing skepticism about his intentions.

As the discussion progressed, Rastignac inadvertently suggested that Madame de Nucingen was the intended recipient of the letter, causing a visible reaction in Madame de Listomere. She dismissed him coldly, pointing out the absurdity of the situation. Feeling foolish, Rastignac left after a few awkward exchanges.

Days later, Madame de Listomere withdrew from society, claiming illness, while the marquis attributed her absence to stomach inflammation. In reality, she was experiencing a nervous attack, using it as an excuse to avoid social interactions.

The narrative highlights the complexities of social dynamics and misunderstandings in romantic pursuits among the young and inexperienced.
