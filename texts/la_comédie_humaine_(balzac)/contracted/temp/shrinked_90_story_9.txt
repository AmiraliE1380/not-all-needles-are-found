The Marquise de Listomere, embodying Restoration values, balances virtue and elegance in her unremarkable marriage. Despite her reserved nature, she commands respect and engages thoughtfully with men. A dance with Eugène de Rastignac sparks her curiosity, leading her to reflect on societal expectations and her desires after receiving his bold letter.

################################################
Madame de Listomere forbids her porter from admitting Rastignac and burns a love letter meant for her. At a party, she anticipates his arrival but realizes he won't come, leading to her emotional turmoil. 

Rastignac later discovers he mistakenly sent her a love letter and regrets not visiting her sooner. When he finally does, Madame feigns offense but seems amused, revealing tension between them. A misunderstanding about the letter's intended recipient causes her to dismiss him coldly. 

She subsequently withdraws from society, claiming illness, while actually experiencing a nervous attack, highlighting the complexities of social dynamics and romantic misunderstandings.
