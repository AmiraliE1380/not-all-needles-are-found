In Alencon, Chevalier de Valois, a 58-year-old gentleman, dreams of marrying a wealthy old maid to improve his status. Suzanne, a young girl working for Madame Lardot, seeks to escape provincial life for Paris, and the chevalier encourages her to pursue local bachelor Monsieur du Bousquier. Meanwhile, Madame Granson struggles to support her son Athanase, who loves his wealthy cousin, Mademoiselle Cormon. His feelings evolve from financial interest to genuine affection, complicated by societal expectations.

Mademoiselle Cormon, childless and single, projects maternal instincts onto her mare, Penelope, while her household dynamics involve loyal servants Jacquelin, Josette, and Mariette. Cormon hopes to attract a suitable husband but struggles with meaningful conversation, often relying on the chevalier for humor. When she contemplates her future, her hopes are dashed upon learning that Monsieur de Troisville, a potential suitor, is married.

After accepting du Bousquier's proposal to regain dignity, Athanase, devastated by the news, withdraws into despair and ultimately takes his own life. His death shocks the community, revealing the tragic consequences of unfulfilled love and societal pressures. Du Bousquier's marriage alters the town's dynamics, showcasing the complexities of ambition and personal tragedy intertwined with social expectations.
