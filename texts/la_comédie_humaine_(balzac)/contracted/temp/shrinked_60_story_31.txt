De Balzac dedicates "The Vicar of Tours" to sculptor David, highlighting the lasting nature of David's art compared to his own transient writing. 

In autumn 1826, Abbe Birotteau, a corpulent priest nearing sixty, hopes for a canon nomination at Saint-Gatien Cathedral. He lives with Mademoiselle Gamard, an old maid who boards priests, and envies the apartment of his late friend, Abbe Chapeloud. Initially grateful for Gamard's care, Birotteau soon feels neglected and suspects her ill will, blinded by ambition. 

As tensions rise, Birotteau forgets previous discord. When his servant is absent, Abbe Troubert expresses concern, unaware of Birotteau's naivety regarding his ambitions. Birotteau discovers Gamard's plot against him, feeling tormented and isolated. Despite warnings, he naively signs a document relinquishing his belongings, allowing Gamard to claim them. Confronting her, he realizes Troubert has taken over his apartment. After Gamard's death, her fortune goes to Troubert, consolidating his power while Birotteau's life deteriorates amid illness and despair.
