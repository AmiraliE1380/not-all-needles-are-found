**The Ball at Sceaux - Summary**

The story revolves around the Comte de Fontaine, a staunch royalist from Poitou who, despite his noble lineage and past sacrifices for the Bourbon cause, finds himself struggling in post-revolutionary France. After moving to Paris with his family, he receives unexpected honors, including a military promotion and royal decorations, which reignite his hopes for royal favor. However, he soon realizes that his sacrifices have gone unrecognized, leading to disillusionment with the new regime.

The narrative shifts focus to his daughter, Emilie de Fontaine, a beautiful and spoiled young woman who has grown up in luxury and is now of marriageable age. Despite numerous suitors, she remains indifferent, holding out for a match that meets her lofty standards of nobility. Her father, while supportive, is increasingly concerned about her stubbornness and pride.

Emilie attends a ball at Sceaux, where she encounters Maximilien Longueville, a striking young man who captivates her attention. Initially, she is drawn to him, but her pride leads her to question his social standing. Longueville, who is of noble descent but has recently taken on a banking role, is unaware of Emilie's prejudices against those in trade. Their relationship develops amidst misunderstandings and societal expectations, with Emilie torn between her feelings for Longueville and her desire for a more traditionally noble match.

As their romance blossoms, Emilie learns of Longueville's true background, which complicates her feelings. Despite her initial disdain for those of lower social status, she finds herself deeply in love with him. However, her pride and societal pressures lead her to reject him, believing she must marry someone of higher rank.

Longueville, heartbroken, departs for Italy, leaving Emilie to grapple with her emotions. She attempts to move on, but her heart remains with him. Eventually, she marries another nobleman, the Comte de Kergarouet, but the union lacks the passion she felt for Longueville. 

Years later, at a social gathering, Emilie encounters Longueville again, now a peer of France. The reunion stirs up old feelings and regrets, highlighting the consequences of her earlier choices. Emilie's journey reflects the struggles between love, pride, and societal expectations, ultimately questioning the true nature of happiness and fulfillment in relationships.

The story concludes with Emilie realizing the emptiness of her current life, as she reflects on the love she lost due to her vanity and societal pressures, leaving her with a sense of melancholy and longing for what might have been.
