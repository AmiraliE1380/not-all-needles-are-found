On a bright April morning in 1813, a cabriolet arrives at the Tuileries, where Julie is captivated by a military review commanded by Napoleon. After gaining entry with her father's help, she becomes infatuated with Colonel Victor d’Aiglemont, worrying her father. By March 1814, they are married but strained, and Julie's health declines in their loveless relationship. Despite their daughter Helene, Victor neglects Julie emotionally. She meets Lord Grenville, who becomes infatuated with her, leading to guilt over her feelings. In late 1820, the Marquise d’Aiglemont retreats to Saint-Lange, grappling with grief and societal expectations, while managing her estate as spring approaches.
