Monsieur de Manerville, a former major of the Gate Guards, became miserly after his wife’s death in 1810, restricting his son Paul’s resources. After inheriting a fortune post his father's death, Paul traveled Europe for six years, hoping to attract a partner with his noble title. In winter 1821, he returned to Bordeaux to restore his family mansion and pursue Mademoiselle Evangelista, but both hesitated to express their feelings. 

Jeweler Elie Magus appraised Madame Evangelista's diamonds for the marriage contract, sparking rumors about the marriage's stability. To counter gossip, she organized a lavish "fete of the camellias." As the wedding approached, she worried about the contract's implications for Natalie. Despite her concerns, she proceeded with the signing, vowing to protect her daughter’s interests. 

Five years later, Paul, burdened by debts, left Natalie to rebuild his fortune in Calcutta. Ignoring letters from Monsieur Mathias, he eventually opened them at sea, discovering Natalie’s anguish and a warning of a conspiracy against him, which filled him with anger and resolve.
