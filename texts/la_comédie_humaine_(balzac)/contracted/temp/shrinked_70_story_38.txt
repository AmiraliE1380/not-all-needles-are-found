*Two Poets* is the first part of a trilogy about Lucien, his sister Eve, and their friend David in Angoulême, set during the traditional printing era. The story follows Jérôme-Nicolas Séchard, a former pressman turned greedy businessman, as he navigates his relationship with his son David, who returns from Paris in 1819 to take over the family printing business amid competition from the Cointet Brothers. Tensions rise as Séchard's greed strains their bond. Meanwhile, David and Lucien share artistic dreams, complicating their practical lives. Mme. de Bargeton, seeking freedom from her limiting marriage, becomes entwined with Lucien, facing societal scrutiny.

Lucien, troubled by his feelings for Mme. de Bargeton and his friend David's impact on her reputation, sought success independently, inspired by Napoleon’s meritocracy. He was excited to receive a letter from Mme. de Bargeton, indicating her willingness to help David, despite her biases. David, in love with Lucien's sister Eve, felt unworthy but supported Lucien. During a public reading, Lucien struggled to connect with the audience, resulting in indifference to his poem. Meanwhile, Eve and David's bond deepened, with David proposing marriage. Lucien's status rose after praise from the Bishop and Mme. de Bargeton, but rumors about his closeness with Louise led to a duel challenge. Following a shooting incident involving Bargeton, public sentiment turned against Lucien, leaving him torn between family obligations and his desires as he prepared to leave for Paris.
