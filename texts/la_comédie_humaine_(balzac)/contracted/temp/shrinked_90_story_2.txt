The Comte de Fontaine, a loyal royalist, faced financial ruin after the Revolution and moved to Paris with his family. Despite political success, he struggled to find a suitable husband for his difficult daughter, Emilie, whose high standards alienated suitors and strained family dynamics amid shifting political fortunes.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
Emilie de Fontaine, a beautiful and proud young woman, struggles with her sense of entitlement and unrealistic standards for suitors, alienating potential partners. Her father, concerned about her stubbornness, watches as she dismisses wealthy men. At a social event, she meets Maximilien Longueville, igniting her hopes for romance. Emilie's journey highlights the challenges young women face in balancing societal expectations with personal desires.
################################################
############# MIDDLE OF THE STORY. #############
################################################
The next morning, Emilie's uncle, the Comte de Kergarouet, apologized to Monsieur Longueville for his previous rudeness and invited him to dinner, promising introductions to beautiful women. Longueville accepted, charmed by the Count's demeanor. At the Villa Planat, he impressed Emilie's family but declined dinner due to his sister's health. As Emilie grew fond of Longueville, she sought to learn more about him, organizing a dance to meet his sister, Clara. Despite her father's concerns about Longueville's background, Emilie dismissed them, believing in love's power. However, discovering Longueville working in a shop shattered her idealized image, leading to emotional turmoil. Eventually, she married the Count but remained haunted by her feelings for Longueville, realizing their connection lingered even years later.
