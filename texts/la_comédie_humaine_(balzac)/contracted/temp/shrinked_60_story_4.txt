In October 1800, Bartolomeo di Piombo confronted the Bonapartes outside the Tuileries Palace, burdened by hardship. After revealing he had killed the Portas, responsible for his son’s death, Napoleon offered him a chance to live in Paris, which Bartolomeo accepted, pledging loyalty to the Bonaparte family.

Fifteen years later, Bartolomeo's life in Paris revolved around themes of vengeance and loyalty. Servin, an artist, opened a studio for wealthy girls, including Ginevra Piombo, who faced ostracism but remained dedicated to her art. Despite Amelie's attempts to undermine her, Ginevra excelled and formed a connection with Luigi, a hidden officer. As rumors spread, Ginevra focused on her feelings for Luigi, while he remained loyal to his beliefs. She sought to help him secure a pardon, highlighting loyalty, love, and societal judgment.

“Here she is, my Ginevra!” Piombo exclaimed, embracing his daughter. He noted her long hours at the studio, suggesting painting had become more important than family. Ginevra shocked her parents by confessing her love for Luigi Porta, a proscribed comrade. Piombo insisted she should love no one but him, but Ginevra argued that love is not a choice.

Despite Piombo's anger and threats against marrying a Porta, Ginevra remained resolute, asserting Luigi's innocence. Confined at home, she communicated with Luigi through a chambermaid. On her birthday, when notaries arrived to discuss her marriage, Piombo cast her out, declaring, “You have no father.” Ginevra sought refuge with Madame Servin, who refused to help, and eventually rented a room near Luigi.

On their wedding day, they felt the absence of their families. As winter progressed, financial strain grew despite Ginevra's commendable paintings. Their situation worsened with the birth of their son, Bartolomeo. In despair, Luigi decided to sell himself as a military substitute. Rushing home, he found Ginevra holding their lifeless child. Ginevra succumbed to exhaustion and died in his arms, leaving him devastated. Meanwhile, her parents were haunted by memories of their daughter, leading to profound grief over their loss.
