**La Grenadière** by Honoré de Balzac, translated by Ellen Marriage, depicts a charming house on the Loire River near Tours, surrounded by cliffs, gardens, and vineyards. The property features terraced gardens, a rose-filled area with a wooden summer-house, and a rustic house with large windows and yellow-washed walls. Inside, a crooked staircase leads to a dining room and simple bedrooms, suggesting it was once a retreat for townsfolk.

In the spring of the Restoration, Mme. Willemsens arrived at La Grenadière with her housekeeper and two children, seeking refuge from town. Her modest furnishings and tall, slender figure with a melancholic expression intrigued locals. Known as Augusta Willemsens, Countess of Brandon, her noble background was a mystery, yet her deep bond with her children, Louis-Gaston and Marie-Gaston, was clear.

Dedicated to her children, Mme. Willemsens fostered a nurturing environment filled with lessons and play. She actively participated in their studies, enjoying lunch in the summer-house while quietly embroidering. Her joy in watching them play often brought tears to her eyes, symbolizing her ideal happiness. However, her health concerns loomed over their idyllic life. One evening, she confided in Louis about her illness, urging him to be strong and promising to guide him in caring for Marie.

As summer progressed, her health declined, casting a shadow over La Grenadière. Despite this, Louis focused on his studies, preparing for the entrance examination to the Ecole polytechnique, encouraged by his mother. In October, she enjoyed tender moments with her children, but her condition worsened, leading to her confinement to her room. There, she played the piano one last time and expressed fears about their future.

Before passing away, she instructed Louis to retrieve important papers and urged him to care for Marie while pursuing a career at sea. Finding solace in his determination, she felt peace before her death. After her funeral, Louis took on the responsibility of caring for Marie as he began an apprenticeship, resolved to honor his mother’s memory and embody her resilience.
