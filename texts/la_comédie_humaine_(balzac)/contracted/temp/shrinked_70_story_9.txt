The Marquise de Listomere represents the Restoration's blend of piety and grace. Married to the dull Marquis, she maintains a virtuous facade. After a dance with Eugène de Rastignac, she burned his letter, surprised her maid. Disappointed by his absence at a party, she discovered he sent her a love letter meant for someone else. Their conversation exposed her doubts about his intentions, prompting her to retreat from society, feigning illness, highlighting the intricacies of romantic misunderstandings.
