In "La Grande Bretèche," Dr. Bianchon recounts the eerie history of a decaying house near Vendôme, once a vibrant estate now shrouded in mystery. The house belonged to the late Comtesse de Merret, whose will forbids entry or repairs for fifty years. As Bianchon learns about her tragic life and isolation, local gossip reveals a once-happy couple's decline and hints at a mysterious young man linked to the Comtesse. The story explores themes of love, loss, and the enduring impact of the past.

A woman narrates the mysterious disappearance of Count Férédia, who left behind money and diamonds for masses after vanishing. She suspects his involvement in Madame de Merret's death, leading her to pursue maid Rosalie for answers. The story reveals Monsieur de Merret's tragic confrontation with his wife, who was hiding a man, culminating in despair and moral conflict.
