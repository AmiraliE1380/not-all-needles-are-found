In Paris, social events are split between superficial official parties and intimate salons that encourage genuine dialogue. Women like Madame d’Espard and Mademoiselle des Touches host these salons, where authentic wit flourishes. 

During one gathering, de Marsay shared a past betrayal, leading to discussions on fidelity and his relationship with Charlotte, which became tense when she accused him of destroying her illusions about love. Blondet criticized modern women's intelligence, while the Princesse de Cadignan argued that true passion remains constant despite societal changes.
