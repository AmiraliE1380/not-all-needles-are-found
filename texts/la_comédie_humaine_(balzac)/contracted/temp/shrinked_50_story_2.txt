The Comte de Fontaine, a royalist from Poitou, faced financial ruin during the French Revolution when his estate was confiscated. He married Mademoiselle de Kergarouet and moved to Paris with his large family, seeking royal favor. Initially promoted to brigadier-general, he felt unrecognized and considered returning to his estate. After March 20, 1815, he became a loyal servant to Louis XVIII, securing positions for his sons.

Despite their improved status, the Comte struggled to find suitable husbands for his three daughters, particularly the youngest, Emilie. Her high standards and spoiled nature made her difficult to please, leading her to dismiss potential suitors over trivial flaws. Emilie, beautiful yet proud, alienated many suitors, and her father's realization of her entitlement came too late.

At a social event, she met Maximilien Longueville, whose elegance intrigued her. As they interacted more, Emilie's affection grew, but she was troubled by his mysterious background. When she discovered him working in a shop, her idealized image shattered, leading to emotional turmoil. Their final exchange before his departure to Italy was filled with unspoken emotions. Months later, Emilie married the Comte de Kergarouet but remained haunted by memories of Longueville and her choices.
