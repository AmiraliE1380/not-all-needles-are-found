In late November 1809, Paris celebrated Napoleon's marriage, contrasting with the Republic's past austerity. Women sought romances with soldiers, while the Comte de Gondreville hosted a lavish gathering for Napoleon, who did not attend due to issues with Josephine. The story follows General Montcornet, Baron Martial de la Roche-Hugon, and the Comtesse de Vaudremont, with jealousy brewing over a mysterious woman. Soulanges, envious of Martial's interest, risks his fortune gambling. The evening ends with Martial attempting to seduce the Countess, only to discover the diamond ring he offers belongs to her husband, highlighting themes of love, jealousy, and societal expectations.
