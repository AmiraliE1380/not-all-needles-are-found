On a rainy March morning in Rue Saint-Denis, a young man admired the whimsical shop "Cat and Racket." His thoughts were interrupted by playful apprentices and a beautiful girl, Augustine, who stirred feelings of loss when she retreated. Shopkeeper Monsieur Guillaume enforced strict discipline while his eldest apprentice, Joseph Lebas, secretly loved Augustine. After a ball, painter Theodore de Sommervieux sought clandestine meetings with Augustine, frustrated by rivals. Eventually, Guillaume agreed to their marriage after learning of Theodore's respectable background. However, as Augustine expected their first child, Theodore's social life strained their relationship, leading to isolation and tragedy amidst their passionate love.
