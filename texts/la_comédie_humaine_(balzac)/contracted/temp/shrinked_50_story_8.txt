In September 1835, Mademoiselle du Rouvre married Polish exile Comte Adam Mitgislas Laginski. Despite her father's financial troubles, Clementine inherited wealth from her mother’s family. Initially perceived as frivolous, Adam gained respect after buying a house in Paris, becoming a prominent figure by 1836. Captain Thaddeus Paz, a nobleman from Adam's poor branch, managed their finances and was a loyal friend.

Clementine grew curious about the reserved Thaddeus, inviting him to dinner, which frustrated Adam. Thaddeus struggled with unrequited love for Clementine while trying to stabilize the household amid Adam's financial irresponsibility. As their interactions deepened, Clementine began to see Thaddeus differently.

Thaddeus became infatuated with circus performer Malaga, fabricating a story to distract from his feelings for Clementine. Their relationship soured when Malaga confronted him. Meanwhile, Clementine expressed disdain for women like Malaga, while Adam defended Thaddeus, unaware of his secret support for her.

As Thaddeus cared for Adam during an illness, he suppressed his feelings for Clementine, who began to appreciate his management. During carnival season, Thaddeus proposed a night out with Malaga, leading to a confrontation with Clementine, who demanded he end the relationship. Ultimately, Thaddeus decided to leave Paris for an expedition, believing it best for all. After his departure, Clementine realized her feelings for him, while Thaddeus expressed his unrequited love in a letter. Three years later, Clementine remained intrigued by Thaddeus, highlighting the complexities of love and societal expectations.
