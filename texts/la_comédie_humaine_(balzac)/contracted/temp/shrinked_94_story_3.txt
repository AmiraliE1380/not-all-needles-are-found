**The Purse**  
*By Honoré de Balzac*  
*Translated by Clara Bell*

**To Sofka**

In the twilight hour, a young painter, Hippolyte Schinner, lost in contemplation of his nearly finished artwork, fell from a ladder and lost consciousness. He was rescued by two women, Madame Leseigneur and her daughter, Adelaide, who cared for him until he regained his senses. The painter was struck by Adelaide's beauty, which embodied the ideal he had envisioned in his art.

Hippolyte had recently moved into a studio in a modest part of Paris, having gained some fame and financial stability through his talent. His mother, a devoted and self-sacrificing woman, had raised him with love despite their hardships. Now, as he began to enjoy success, he longed to provide her with a better life.

After his accident, Hippolyte became increasingly enamored with Adelaide, feeling a connection between their shared struggles. He visited the Leseigneurs frequently, drawn to their refined manners and the warmth of their company. Despite their apparent poverty, he admired their dignity and grace.

As their relationship blossomed, Hippolyte offered to paint a portrait of Madame Leseigneur, which she accepted with a mix of gratitude and reserve. The painter's feelings for Adelaide deepened, and they shared moments of intimacy and understanding, though neither had yet confessed their love.

However, doubts began to creep into Hippolyte's mind. He overheard gossip about the Leseigneurs, suggesting they were not as respectable as they appeared. A theft of his purse, containing fifteen gold louis, further fueled his suspicions. Despite his love for Adelaide, he struggled with the idea that she might be involved in the theft.

Determined to confront his feelings, Hippolyte returned to the Leseigneurs, only to find Adelaide looking pale and troubled. Their reunion was bittersweet, as he felt guilty for his earlier suspicions. The evening was filled with unspoken emotions, and as they played cards, Hippolyte noticed a new purse that Adelaide had discreetly placed before him, revealing her affection and thoughtfulness.

Just as he was about to propose to Adelaide, the old vice-admiral, a friend of Madame Leseigneur, arrived, bringing with him a sense of familial warmth. The evening ended with a sense of unity and hope, as the characters navigated their complex relationships, revealing the intertwining of love, honor, and societal expectations.

In the end, the story illustrates the delicate balance between love and trust, the impact of social standing on personal relationships, and the transformative power of genuine affection amidst adversity.
