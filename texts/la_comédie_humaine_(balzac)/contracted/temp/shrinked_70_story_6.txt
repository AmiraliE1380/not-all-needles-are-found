The Rue du Tourniquet-Saint-Jean was home to Madame Crochard and her daughter Caroline, who lived modestly. Despite their struggles, Madame Crochard hoped for a brighter future for Caroline, who caught the attention of the "Black Gentleman." Their bond grew over two months, with the Gentleman secretly aiding them financially. In May, Caroline invited him, now Roger, to Montmorency, where their relationship flourished, culminating in a kiss at a village ball. 

Years later in Paris, Caroline and Roger are happy but face challenges, including her ailing mother and social pressures. Meanwhile, Monsieur de Granville, engaged to Mademoiselle Bontems, grapples with marital tensions as Angelique's strictness clashes with his desire for a vibrant life. Their relationship deteriorates, leading to Granville's infidelity and emotional isolation. The story concludes with Granville reflecting on his complex family dynamics as he prepares to leave for Italy.
