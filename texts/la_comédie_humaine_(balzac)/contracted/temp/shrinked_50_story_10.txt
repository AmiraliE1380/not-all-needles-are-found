In Paris, social gatherings are divided into superficial official parties and intimate salons that foster genuine conversation. Hosts like Madame d’Espard and Mademoiselle des Touches create spaces where authentic French wit thrives, contrasting with the coldness of official events.

Mademoiselle des Touches’ salon is celebrated for its grace and open dialogue. One evening, prominent figures, including the charming de Marsay, discussed the evolution of women in France since the July Revolution, highlighting the wit of Parisian social life. As the night unfolded, guests shared personal stories, with Mademoiselle des Touches ensuring everyone remained engaged.

De Marsay recounted a formative experience involving a secret love for an older widow, which ended in betrayal and prompted reflections on love and trust. He later began a relationship with Charlotte, whose beauty reminded him of his past. However, doubts arose when he discovered the artist used false hair for a keepsake, leading to insecurities about love. In a tense conversation with Charlotte, she accused him of destroying her illusions about love, while he maintained that love was a game for men.
