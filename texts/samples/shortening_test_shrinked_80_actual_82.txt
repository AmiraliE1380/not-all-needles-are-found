Issoudun is marked by social stagnation and economic decline, with families isolated and lacking class distinctions. The town's nobility has vanished due to historical upheavals, and it has repeatedly rejected military garrisons, losing both connection to modernity and economic benefits. A notable judicial drama in the past further alienated the town from military presence. Similarly, Bourges suffers from declining vitality, attributed to government negligence. The administration fails to address local issues, as few capable individuals are willing to work in such stagnant regions. By 1822, Issoudun was governed by locals, leading to ineffective administration and a lack of respect for authority, including the clergy.
