Doctor Rouget, who paid for Maxence Gilet's seminary board until 1805, left the question of his paternity unresolved. After Rouget's death in 1806, Max, a reckless youth, engaged in various mischiefs and accidentally caused a pregnant woman's death while stealing fruit. Fleeing to Bourges, he enlisted in the army and quickly rose to captain during three campaigns. Captured in Portugal, he spent four years in the brutal Spanish prison at Cabrera, where he became a dominant figure by eliminating bullies among the prisoners. After returning to Issoudun post-war, he found his parents dead and sought recognition for his military service but faced rejection. Despite his controversial reputation, Max became a local leader among former soldiers, admired by the common people for his bravery, while the bourgeoisie disapproved of him.
